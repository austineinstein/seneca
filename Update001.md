### Key observations for your reflexivity thesis

* The largest drawdowns often followed periods of:

  * excessive leverage,
  * narrative euphoria,
  * liquidity expansion,
  * or macro tightening.

* Many crashes were amplified by **reflexive feedback loops**:

  * rising prices → increased leverage → stronger bullish sentiment → more capital inflows → collapse once confidence breaks.

* Bitcoin historically behaves less like a stable hedge and more like a **high-beta reflexive asset** during systemic stress periods.

* Several drawdowns coincided with broader macro events:

  * COVID crash (2020)
  * aggressive central bank tightening (2022)
  * geopolitical uncertainty
  * liquidity contractions.

### Useful framing for your paper

You could classify drawdowns into 3 categories:

1. **Structural / Internal Failures**

   * Mt. Gox
   * FTX
   * Celsius / 3AC

2. **Macro Liquidity Events**

   * COVID crash
   * Fed tightening cycle

3. **Speculative Reflexive Bubbles**

   * 2011 bubble
   * ICO mania
   * 2021 leverage cycle

A framework that supports: 

* endogenous reflexivity (crypto leverage/speculation),
* from exogenous macro shocks (rates, war, liquidity).
